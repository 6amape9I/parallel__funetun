Architecture

Four Roles, One Trust Loop

Smart contracts, off-chain orchestration, and PyTorch workers deliver training speed with transparent payments.

JobManager Contract

Manages jobs, deposits, registration, and payouts. Stores only hashes but every payment is recorded on-chain.

Orchestrator Service

Assigns micro-tasks, calls validators, and publishes confirmed hashes. Event-driven orchestration for real-time response.

Trainer Client

Executes K local steps on a data shard, computes delta, and sends hash for verification and payment.

Validator Client

Repeats training or performs spot-checks, then signals validity for payout approval.

Aggregation Strategies

Averaging, median, or trimmed mean increase robustness against noisy or malicious deltas.

Incentive Accounting

Deposits are locked upfront, so participants trust automatic payouts after validation.

Lifecycle

From Job to Payout

01

Client Creates Job

Deposit and base reward are locked in the contract, guaranteeing funds for trainers.

02

Orchestrator Splits Work

Micro-tasks are created based on GPU class and model requirements, then assigned to registered trainers.

03

Trainers Compute Deltas

Each worker executes local steps, hashes the delta, and sends the update off-chain plus hash on-chain.

04

Validators Confirm

Spot-check or repeated training verifies updates before signaling the contract.

05

Contract Releases Rewards

Verified updates trigger automatic payouts with transparent tracking.

Trust Loop

Reliability Without Sacrificing Speed

Hash-Based Accounting

On-chain stores hashes and validation results while data remains private.

Validator Gateway

Payouts only proceed after verification, reducing impact of malicious updates.

Event Trail

Every job creation, update, and payout emits an event for audit and analytics.

  --network localhost

  --registry http://localhost:8000 \
